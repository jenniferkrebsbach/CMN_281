**Job Title: Fireman**

**Job Summary:**
A fireman, also known as a firefighter, is a vital member of the emergency services community, dedicated to saving lives, protecting property, and preventing fires. The fireman is trained to respond to emergencies, extinguish fires, rescue individuals in danger, and provide medical assistance when necessary. This job requires physical fitness, quick decision-making, and a strong commitment to community safety. Firemen often work in teams and must be ready to face hazardous and unpredictable situations at a moment's notice.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Respond to emergency calls related to fires, medical emergencies, hazardous materials incidents, and other public safety needs.
- Operate fire apparatus and equipment, including hoses, pumps, ladders, and emergency vehicles.
- Extinguish fires using various firefighting techniques and equipment.
- Conduct rescue operations in various emergency situations, including structural fires, vehicle accidents, and natural disasters.
- Perform fire prevention inspections and community education programs to promote fire safety awareness.
- Participate in regular training sessions to maintain and update firefighting skills and knowledge of techniques and safety protocols.
- Administer first aid and perform CPR on victims as required prior to the arrival of medical personnel.
- Maintain firefighting equipment, tools, and fire stations in a clean and functional condition.
- Collaborate with other emergency services and agencies during major incidents and community events.

**Annual Pay Scale:**

- **Entry Level:** $35,000 - $45,000
- **Mid-Level (3-5 years experience):** $50,000 - $60,000
- **Senior Level (5+ years experience):** $65,000 - $75,000
- **Specialized Roles (e.g. Fire Chief, Training Officer):** $75,000 - $100,000+

**Necessary Qualifications:**

- High school diploma or equivalent; additional training or an associate's degree in fire science or a related field is preferred.
- Completion of a state-approved firefighter training program and certification as a firefighter (e.g., NFPA certification).
- Valid driver's license with a clean driving record; commercial driver's license (CDL) preferred, as operating fire trucks may be required.
- CPR and First Aid certifications, with advanced medical training (EMT/Paramedic) being a significant advantage.
- Strong physical fitness and resilience, with the ability to pass physical agility tests.
- Excellent communication skills and the ability to work effectively in a team environment.
- Willingness to work irregular hours, including nights, weekends, and holidays, as emergencies can occur at any time.
